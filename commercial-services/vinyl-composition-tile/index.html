What is VCT?

VCT is the acronym for Vinyl Composition Tile. VCT flooring is mostly used in commercial establishments like hospitals, schools, professional offices, car dealerships and banks, to name some of the most common. VCT flooring is a smart investment as it is fairly easy to repair and can last for years with the proper maintenance. Regular maintenance performed by a professional with the proper equipment will help to keep your vinyl composition tile floors clean and in the best condition, so you can get the most out of your investment.

How do you maintain vinyl composition tile?

Regular cleaning is only one part of properly maintaining VCT flooring. Another important service to have performed by professionals is the strip and wax. This involves carefully removing the existing layer of finish before adding a new one. This process may sound simple, but it is quite labor intensive and is best handled by professionals with the proper equipment and experience. Team Clean has been maintaining vinyl composition tile flooring for over 15 years and will help you get the most out of your VCT flooring for years to come.

Professional vinyl composition tile floor services

Hiring a professional who specializes in maintaining vinyl composition tile floor will help you avoid undesired results and preserve the integrity of your vct flooring. The process of strip and wax services starts with the removal of the existing finish. This must be done carefully to not damage the tile and with special attention to lobbies, entryways, and other areas that experience high traffic. Once the finish is removed and the tiles are dry, the tiles are buffed until they glisten. Team Clean has years of experience maintaining vinyl composition tile and can help keep your floor in the best condition.

How often should I strip and wax my floors?

The answer depends on a number of things. What type of business do you operate? What kind of weather do you experience? How much traffic does your business have on a daily basis? Naturally, the more people you have or the more inclement weather that you experience, the dirtier your vinyl composition tile floors will get. Looking deeper than the appearance of your flooring, dirt, dust and other debris will slowly erode your floors’ delicate finish. Left untreated, this can cause more serious problems in the long run.
